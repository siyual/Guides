# Arebati V25

It's often a toss-up between which is a harder fight between Mboze and Arebati.  And honestly, they're both extremely difficult.  While Mboze's gimmick is that if he ever gets enough TP he will end your entire run due to 4 of 6 of his TP moves results in a full wipe, Arebati's difficulty is in his sheer unapproachability.  Unless you are the tank, you do not want to be *anywhere* near Arebati.

That said, this is going to be a two Key Item approach.  The winning composition we used was the following:

```
KI-1 : WHM SMN BST DNC WAR DRG
KI-2 : BRD COR RNG RUN PLD GEO
```

**Note:** The DDs for the first KI are very interchangable.  The goal for the first KI is simply to get Arebati to at least 74%.  However you can best accomplish that using a melee method without expending your A-team jobs is good here.  We've cleared this using a polearm **SAM** with `Yoichinoyumi` instead of a **WAR**.

## Key Item #1

The goal of the first Key Item is to get it to 74% or lower.  69% or lower is certainly ideal, but not required for this fight.

There is no tank for this.  The tank will be whoever has hate - which most likely will be the **DNC**.  The **WHM** needs to make sure everyone is healthy, and that everyone has `Auspice`.

The **SMN** and **BST** will be coordinating TP wipes between `Cait Sith` and `Fatso Fargann`.

Everyone engaged needs to be in full `Subtle Blow` gear.  The name of the game here is dealing damage without letting him get TP moves off.  This is much more difficult than it was with Mboze, but the difference here is that if he does get some TP moves off, it's not the end of the world.

If he gets off **Polar Roar**, the mechanic to dispel the fetters is doing critical hits.  Just keep on doing what you're doing.

Stay alive and get him down below 74%.  Plan your jobs out accordingly.  If you can afford using some SPs in the first Key Item, by all means, rip it!

## Key Item #2

What?  This wasn't hard enough for you?  Boy howdy do I have some fantastic news for you!  Buckle up, Buttercup, it's about to get frosty.

You might have noticed earlier that the party composition for the second Key Item was a bit... interesting.  For every clear I've done, this is by far the safest and most consistent setup:

> BRD COR RNG RUN PLD GEO

### Buffs

**COR** : Crooked `Chaos`, `Samurai`\
**GEO** : `Indi-Fury`, `Geo-Frailty`\
**BRD** : **Soul Voice**, **Clarion Call** (Do not use NiTro yet)
1. `Minuet 5`
2. `Minuet 4`
3. `Minuet 3` (Or `Aria`)
4. `Honor March`
5. `Dirge`
6. `Ballad 3` → **GEO**

**PLD**: `Protect`, `Shell`, `PLD stuff`

Once ready, the **RUN** goes in to claim **Arebati** and pull it to the right side of the arena, the **PLD** needs to claim the add and tank it at the top of the stairs, and everyone else needs to be on the left side of the arena.

The **PLD** needs to be close enough to the **RUN** and to the rest of the party to `Cure` them, but not close enough to take AoE damage from **Arebati**.  The **PLD** is going to be extremely busy this fight.  They will be tanking both adds, *and* they will be the main healer.  

**RNG** should use `Annihilator`, and alternate between using `Coronach` and `Slug Shot`

**COR** should use `Fomalhaut`, and alternate between using `Detonator` and `Last Stand`

Back to the fight, though: when there is 60 seconds remaining on **Soul Voice**, the **BRD** needs to use `NiTro` and resing:

DDs:
1. `Minuet 5`
2. `Minuet 4`
3. `Minuet 3` (or `Aria`)
4. `Honor March`
5. `Dirge`
6. `Ballad 3` → **GEO**

Move to the **PLD**:
1. `Minne 5`
2. `Minne 4`
3. `Ballad 3`
4. `Ballad 2`
5. `Victory March`

Debuffs:
1. `Nocturne` → **Arebati**
2. `Elegy` → **Arebati**
3. `Elegy` → **Adds**

Stand on the opposite side of the **RUN** close enough to **Pianissimo** but still out of AoE range:
1. `Ice Carol` → **RUN**
2. `Victory March` → **RUN**
3. `Ice Carol II` → **RUN**
4. `Foe Sirvente` → **RUN**

The **BRD** *needs* to have dedicated macros for singing pp songs on the **RUN**.  They will be **constantly** dispelled.  The **BRD** will basically just be on **RUN** rebuffing duty the entire fight.  It is **highly recommended** that the **BRD** uses the `PartyBuffs` addon with colored song icons to quickly identify what needs to be reapplied. 

Keep an eye on the **RUN**'s MP as well, and substitute in a `Ballad` if necessary.  `Carol` and `Sirvente` is the highest importance.

The **RUN** is going to basically just be blind this fight.  The fetters are just a super bright light show, but otherwise not too dangerous unless caught after a full dispel.  The **RUN** needs to have at least 3 stacks of `Panacea`, `Remedies`, and `Vile Elixirs`.  Not much to say here.  Keep hate and survive.

When the second add pops, the **PLD** needs to be ready to claim the add immediately.  They should already be second on the hate list, since the **RUN** has `Sirvente` and the DDs have `Dirge`, but just in case, be ready to claim.  Rinse & repeat, only with a new challenger in the arena.

**PLD** is the main healer, and also tanking both adds.  They need to be damn good at their job.

`Yaegasumi` is completely irrelevant for ranged Weapon Skills.  Don't lose tempo, and keep pumping damage.

**GEO** should try to get `Impact` on when they can, but it likely won't last too long.

**Bolster**, **Overkill**, **Wild Card**, and **Random Deal** with coordination.  This fight is very, very hard.  

Keep at it, and eventually you'll best him!  And then congrats, you beat the game! (Sort of).
